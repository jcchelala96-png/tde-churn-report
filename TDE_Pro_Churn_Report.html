Churn Feedback ‚Äî Key Takeaways

‚úÖ Product-Market Fit Is Strong

85% of churned members left with positive or neutral sentiment. Many explicitly said the product is "amazing" and they plan to return. This is not a product problem ‚Äî it's a life-circumstances problem.

üí° "Mike" Needs Bite-Sized Content

Your ideal user (Mike, busy 9-to-5) is exactly who's churning due to time constraints. Consider: daily 5-min digests, audio summaries for commuting, "start here" quick paths, and a weekly email with the top 3 things to focus on.

‚ö†Ô∏è DYD ‚Üí TDE Pro Transition Needs Work

Multiple DYD alumni felt TDE Pro content overlaps too much with DYD, or didn't match their expectation of "more DeFi strategies." Clearly differentiating TDE Pro from DYD in marketing and onboarding could reduce this friction.

üåç Language Barrier Is a Silent Killer

At least 3 members (11%) cited language as a primary factor. They liked the product but couldn't engage with English-heavy video content. Subtitles, transcripts, or Spanish translations could unlock a retained segment.

‚ö†Ô∏è Content Overload Feeds the "No Time" Problem

Members feel overwhelmed by the volume of content ‚Äî too many tabs, too many videos, unclear where to start. Streamlining the onboarding and adding a "what to focus on this week" curation layer could directly address the top churn reason.

üí° Pause Program Could Save Revenue

8 members requested pauses instead of cancellation. Many "No Time" churners said they'll return. A formal pause option (e.g., 1-3 months freeze at reduced rate) could retain members instead of losing them entirely.
